First Time Boarding? Here's What You Need

✅ What to Bring

  • ✓ Vaccination records (rabies, DHPP, bordetella)
  • ✓ Food in sealed container (enough for stay + 2 days)
  • ✓ Medications with clear instructions
  • ✓ Favorite toy or blanket
  • ✓ Collar with ID tag
  • ✓ Leash and harness
  • ✓ Emergency contact numbers
  • ✓ Treats (if your dog has favorites)

❌ What NOT to Bring

  • ✗ Rawhide chews
  • ✗ Frayed or damaged toys
  • ✗ Glass bowls
  • ✗ Valuable items
  • ✗ Large bags of food (we'll store properly)
  • ✗ Electric items or heating pads
  • ✗ Other pets

Frequently Asked Questions

Booking & Reservations

How far in advance should I book? +
We recommend booking 2-4 weeks in advance for weekends and holidays. Weekdays can often be booked with 48 hours notice. For peak seasons (Chinese New Year, Christmas, summer holidays), book at least 1-2 months ahead.
What is your cancellation policy? +
Free cancellation up to 48 hours before check-in. Cancellations within 48 hours incur a 50% charge. No-shows are charged in full. For extended stays (2+ weeks), 7 days notice required.
Can I book a custom package? +
Yes! Contact us to discuss your dog's specific needs. We offer custom packages for extended stays, senior dogs, puppies, medical needs, and special requirements. We'll create a personalized care plan and provide a quote within 24 hours.

Health & Safety

What vaccines are required? +
Current rabies, DHPP (distemper, hepatitis, parainfluenza, parvovirus), and bordetella (kennel cough) vaccines are required. Proof must be provided at check-in. We recommend updating vaccines at least 1 week before stay.
What if my dog needs medication? +
Our 24-hour vet team can administer medications at no extra cost. Please provide medications in original containers with clear instructions (dosage, time, method). We'll document each administration.
What if my dog gets sick during their stay? +
Our 24-hour on-call vet handles all medical issues immediately. We contact you right away, explain the situation, and follow your instructions for treatment. For emergencies, we'll transport to our partner 24-hour animal hospital.

During the Stay

Can I visit during my dog's stay? +
We find visits can be disruptive for dogs and may cause separation anxiety. Instead, we offer video calls and daily photo updates (Premium package). For extended stays (2+ weeks), we can arrange a mid-stay visit.
How do I get daily updates? +
Premium package includes daily photos and videos via email or app. Classic package includes updates every 2 days. You can also purchase photo updates for any package ($15/day). Webcam access available for Premium guests.
Can siblings share a room? +
Yes! Dogs from the same household can share a room for a discounted rate (20% off for second dog). Contact us for pricing. We'll ensure they have enough space and can be together or separate as needed.

Breeds & Special Needs

Do you take large breeds like Dobermans? +
Absolutely! We love all breeds. Our facilities include extra-large rooms, orthopedic beds sized for large dogs, secure paddocks with 6-foot fencing, and staff trained in handling all breeds. We've boarded over 45 Dobermans in the past year with 100% satisfaction.
Do you accept unvaccinated puppies? +
Yes, with vet approval and proof of first vaccinations. Puppies have separate play areas from adult dogs, more frequent potty breaks, and gentle introduction to socializing. Ask about our Puppy Package.
What about dogs with separation anxiety? +
We're experienced with anxious dogs. We offer extra one-on-one attention, calming music, pheromone diffusers, and can arrange video calls. Let us know in advance so we can prepare a calm environment.

Seasonal Care Tips for Hong Kong

☀️

Summer (May-Sep)

All outdoor activities in early morning/late evening

Indoor play during peak heat

Constant fresh water and cooling mats

Booties for hot pavement

🌧️

Typhoon Season

All dogs moved to interior safe rooms

Storm anxiety protocols in place

Calming music and extra comfort

24-hour staffing during weather events

❄️

Winter (Dec-Feb)

Underfloor heating activated

Cozy blankets on all beds

Shorter walks in cold weather

Indoor play prioritized
